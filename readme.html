Typhoon Track Forecast Service

  Every summer, South Korea experiences significant damage from typhoons. With global warming causing an increase in typhoon intensity, accurate prediction of the typhoon's path and strength at the early stages is crucial. Providing sufficient lead time for response measures can help prevent potential damages.

  For instance, the typhoon HINNAMNOR, which hit South Korea from August to September in 2022, highlights the importance of precise path prediction systems. If the path of typhoon HINNAMNOR had been accurately predicted, the damages could have been mitigated.

  Our project targets this issue by constructing a typhoon track prediction using FourCastNet(FCN), a weather forecasting model based on the Fourier Neural Operator (FNO). Our team focused on studying the integration of FCN, which was trained on European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts Reanalysis v5 data(ERA5 data), with the JMA Best Track data. This effort aims to harness the robust predictive power of FCN and the highly accurate historical records of the JMA to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of our weather forecasting model.

  If you are interested in typhoon prediction using deep learning models, visit our website. It provides not only fundamental information about typhoon path prediction but also detailed insights into how our model operates. This information will enhance the understanding of typhoons and raise awareness of how we can protect ourselves from their impacts.
